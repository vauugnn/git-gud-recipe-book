🍲 Tinolang Manok (Filipino Ginger Chicken Soup)

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces (thighs or drumsticks work best)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2–3 inches ginger, peeled and sliced into matchsticks
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (or to taste), plus more for seasoning
  • 5–6 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 small green papaya or chayote, peeled and sliced
  • 1–2 cups chili leaves (dahon ng sili) or malunggay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 chicken bouillon cube, 1 long green chili (siling haba)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium. Sauté onion until soft. Add garlic and ginger. Cook until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken pieces. Sear for 3–4 minutes until lightly browned. Season with fish sauce.
  3. Pour in water or broth. Bring to a boil, then lower to a simmer. Skim off any foam.
  4. Simmer for 18–22 minutes until chicken is cooked through and tender.
  5. Add green papaya or chayote. Cook for 5–7 minutes until just tender.
  6. Stir in chili leaves or malunggay and the long green chili if using. Cook for 1–2 minutes.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Serve hot with steamed rice. 🍚

Pro Tips

  • Use bone-in chicken for deeper flavor. You can debone after simmering if you prefer.
  • Do not overcook the papaya. Keep it slightly firm for better texture.
  • Ginger is the star. Add more if you want a brighter, warmer broth.
  • Swap greens with spinach or pechay if chili leaves are not available.
  • For extra umami, add a splash of patis right before serving.
